The Inuktut languages of Nunavut and Nunavik have had a very strong connection to and use of the Syllabics since the mid-19th century. While many Inuk communities in Nunavut use Syllabics as their primary and preferred writing system, it is important to note that not all Inuk communities in the Arctic use Syllabics to write their language. The chart below is a pan-Inuktut Syllabics chart, which represents the Syllabics orthographies of communities in Eastern and Western Nunavut, as well as Nunavik, who require the additional "ai" (á‚ á° á‘) and "aai" (á á¯ á‘Œ) vowels, as well as the local "h" series (á•´ á•µ á•¶ á•· á•¸ á•¹ á•º).

> **Note on nomenclature:** Inuk speakers and communities prefer the spelling "[Inuktut](https://tusaalanga.ca/index.php/)" for their language name, as opposed to the persistent "Inuktitut", which is the common English spelling of the language.
